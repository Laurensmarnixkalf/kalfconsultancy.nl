          The user experience

          Most companies give far too little attention to usability in every type of product (especially enterprise software!) but, with a (consumer) Internet service, there simply is no getting around that it’s really all about the user experience. If you can’t find a way to make your service something that the target user can figure out how to use—and provide them with an idea of why they should use it—then you’re going nowhere fast. Also remember that performance is a key factor in usability. If a page takes too long to load, people will think it’s broken or just go elsewhere because the experience is bad.

          Engineers vs. UX design

          It’s important to keep in mind that engineers are usually very poor at user experience design. That’s mostly because they have to think in terms of implementation models, while users think in terms of conceptual models. Make sure that you pay attention to both the interaction design and the visual design. Creating an awesome design does not magically happen while sitting at a desk, it happens over many iterations and feedback loops with real users. That is why the high fidelity prototype is so important. If you can get through at least 5 consecutive user tests with your high fidelity prototype where each individual was able to complete all of the proposed tasks, you’re generally good to go.

          “The design is not something you sprinkle on top when engineering is done.”

          Collaboration from the start

          In order to get to a good user experience design, there needs to be close collaboration between the user experience designer(s) and the product manager. See this close collaboration as a constant mix of input from the different roles where the goal is to discover the blend of requirements and design that meets the needs of the user. The end result needs to be both usable and valuable.

          TL;DR

          You'll see the best results when you work towards an optimal user experience from the start.
