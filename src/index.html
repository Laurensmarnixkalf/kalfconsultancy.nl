          Welcome!

          Product management

          Because it is awesome to create value, to solve problems and to build a solution to those problems. Something that changes people's lives, makes people, happy, and something that lasts. That's what I like about product management.
          It's easy to forget what you've learned to solve a certain problem or to just take it for granted. For that reason, I want an outlet for the things I've learned in product management. This is that place.

          Differentiating 'signal' from 'noise'

          In the modern world it's a really important skill to be able to differentiate 'signal' from 'noise'. The majority of the things that we do on a daily basis, do not move the needle forward, they do not make our boat go faster. The minimalistic approach to product management is about limiting the time and resources you spent on things that don't add value (e.g. maintenance tasks) and maximizing the time and resources you spent on adding value.

          In the end it's all about humans and emotion

          I have a background in Psychology, maybe not the first education path you think of when you consider product management. But it's definitely not a bad place to start from. Studying human behaviour, emotions, how the mind works, group dynamics and 'how to do' a proper (clinical) assessment, all comes in handy when you want to solve problems for people through a product.
