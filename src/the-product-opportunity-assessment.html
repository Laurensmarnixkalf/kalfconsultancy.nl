          The Product Opportunity Assessment

          It's generally a good idea to look before you leap. As a product manager you constantly have to make decisions whether something is worth it to pursue. Fortunately, it's really not that hard to do a useful opportunity assessment.

          Fundamental questions

          Here are the fundamental questions you need to answer to get to a valuable assessment:

          • Exactly what problem will this solve? (value proposition)

          • For whom do we solve that problem? (target market)

          • How big is the opportunity? (market size)

          • What alternatives are out there now? (competitive landscape)

          • Why are we best suited to pursue this? (our differentiator)

          • Why now? (market window)

          • How will we get this product to market? (go-to-market strategy)

          • What factors are critical for success? (solution requirements)

          • Given the above, what is the recommendation? (Go or No-go)

          Answering the question about the value proposition is often the hardest. Can you explain in a few short sentences what the problem is you are trying to solve?

          The why

          The goal of the product opportunity assessment is two-fold.

          1. Prevent the company from wasting time and money on poor opportunities.

          2. For good opportunities, get focus in the team and create understanding for what will be required to succeed and how to define that success later on.

          TL;DR

          It's important to realize that it's just as important to identify bad product ideas as it is to identify good ones. This will prevent the company from wasting precious time and resources. Eliminating an opportunity = a win!
