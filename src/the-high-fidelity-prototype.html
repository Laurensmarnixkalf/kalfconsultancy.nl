          The high fidelity prototype

          In my personal experience, writing lengthy specs is not something that designers and engineers are particularly fond of. Next to that, the question is whether a written document (in whatever tool or form) is the right way to communicate about a digital product.

          Some hints

          Here are some requirements, pun intended, for a good and useful product spec:

          • It must describe the full user experience.

          • It must accurately represent the behabior of the product.

          • It must communicate the behavior of the product in such a way that it can be understood by different types of colleagues (engineer, QA customer service, marketing etc.)

          • It will change, so it must be welcoming to alterations.

          • There needs to be a single representation of the entire thing to reduce confusion and ambiguity.

          Critical

          The high fidelity prototype is a crucial aspect of almost every phase of product development. During product discovery a high fidelity prototype is lightweight enough to iterate on or to throw away completely. Also, the high fidelity prototype gives an engineer the opportunity to get a feel for the scope of the project. She can come up with educated guesses of the size and level of difficulty of the actual implementation. If needed she can already start research on certain aspects she's uncertain about. This can be of great help during a product opportunity assessment. By that time engineering will already have a general idea of what's coming and will be able to give better estimations on how much time will be needed.

          Deliver the full experience

          During the execution phase, where the engineering team starts building the product, nothing beats a high fidelity prototype to deliver the full experience of what we are aiming to achieve, to the team. A clickable prototype allows the developers to click through the different interaction elements, inspect the individual elements and get a good feeling of the overall task(s) that the product wants to achieve. There are many options available today that allow for quick feedback (and even small discussions) in the prototype.

          TL;DR

          Writing books full of specs does not move the needle faster. It is time-consuming and it doesn't make anyone happy. Create a high-fidelity prototype to show the entire thing to everyone who wants to see it.
