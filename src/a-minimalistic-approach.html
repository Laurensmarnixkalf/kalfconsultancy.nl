          A minimalistic approach

          It's not about getting rid of everything, it's about making room for the things that matter. In product, design and development. Getting rid of what doesn't add value, makes room for what does. In the modern world it's a really important skill to be able to differentiate signal from noise. The majority of the things that we do on a daily basis, do not move the needle forward, they do not make our boat go faster. The minimalistic approach to product management is about limiting the time and resources you spent on 'maintenance tasks' and maximizing the time and resources you spent on adding value. Maintenance tasks are the things you need to do, to keep going, but won't actually take you further, they dont add value. Don't get me wrong about those maintenance tasks. They are important, they need to happen. But its an easy trap to fall into, if all youre doing on a day to day basis is maintenance.

          How to

          This might seem easier said than done. So how to go about it? Here are some tips.

          • Ask yourself: is this really important? Can I eliminate it?

          • Automate everything you can.

          • Re-consider time and human-resource heavy activities. Is it necessary? Can we organise it differently?

          • See if you can outsource. Figure out exactly what you need to have done, document it properly and give it to somebody (something) else.

          "Don't get bogged down, focus on what adds value. Make the boat go faster."

          TL;DR

          Only include the stuff that truly adds value. Don't worry about losing "important" things. Less is absolutely more, in moste cases. ;)
